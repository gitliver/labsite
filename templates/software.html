Pandora is a tool for the identification and discovery of tumor-associated microbes via RNAseq.

Pegasus is a tool for the annotation and prediction of oncogenic gene fusions in RNAseq.

GAMToC uses copy number and mutation data from a cohort of patients, for example from TCGA, to find modules of genes with a related mutation pattern.

Genome Browser is the Rabadan Lab's locally hosted implementation of the UCSC Genome Browser. Currently, it features the mm9 exotome and various annotated non-human primate assemblies.

SAVI is a program for calling variants in high-throughput sequencing data, particularly paired tumor-normal samples.


Note: These projects may be in active development and varying stages of usability.
